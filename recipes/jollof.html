Jollof

Home

Jollof Rice is one of the most common one-pot dishes in West Africa.Jollof can be enjoyed as is, but many people will compliment the dish with chicken, beef, fish, or even a salad.

Ingredients

  • 1 large onion white, diced
  • 5 cloves Garlic, shredded
  • 1/4 cup fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 large tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil (vegetable or canola)
  • 2 tsp curry powder (Jamaican style)
  • 1 can of 29 oz tomato sauce
  • 1 hot pepper (Habanero or scotch bonnet)
  • 1 and 1/2 tsp ground chile pepper (cayenne)
  • 1 and 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of chicken stock
  • 2 cups uncooked basmati or Jasmine rice, rinsed

Steps

  1. Dice the onions, shred the garlic, shred the ginger, and slice tomatoes then set aside.
  2. Add the oil to a large pot over low to medium heat and add the sliced onions to the pot and cook until transparent.
  3. Add in the garlic, ginger and curry powder and stir for another 3 minutes.
  4. Add in the sliced tomatoes and cook until soft for about 5-10 minutes, continuously stirring while cooking.
  5. Pour in the tomato sauce, whole hot pepper, ground chile pepper, and salt.
  6. In a separate large pot add 2 cups of the cooked sauce (reserve the rest), broth, water and bring it to a boil.
  7. Stir in the rice until well mixed, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the rice cook for 15-20 minutes or more if needed
